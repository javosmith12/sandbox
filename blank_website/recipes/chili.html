Chili

Description

This soul-southing chili is the one! Great umami flavor with the beef, beans, and a perfect blend of herbs and spices.

  • 1-2lbs of grass-fed beef
  • 1 1/2 cup of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup of diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup of beer
  • 1/4 cup of worcestershire sauce
  • 1/2 cup of wine
  • 1 cup of tomato paste
  • 1 cup of sour cream
  • 1 cup of red kidney beans
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1-2 Anaheim chile peppers
  • 2 jalapeno peppers
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 tablespoons of cumin
  • 2 tablespoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of hot sauce
  • 2 teaspoons of dried basil
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of smoked paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 2 tablespoons of dried cilantro
  • salt and pepper, and garlic powder to taste
  1. On medium-high heat, add couple tablespoons of oil and saute onion, garlic, Anaheim, bell, and jalapeno peppers until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. On medium-high heat, add a teaspoon or two of oil to a another pan and cook beef until browned and crumbly, about 6-8 minutes.
  3. Add worcestershire sauce and garlic powder, and beer. Continue to cook , scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the skillet, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in beef with peppers and add crushed and diced tomatoes, and wine. Season with chili powder, cumin, brown sugar, hot saud, basil, paprika, salt, oregano, and black pepper.
  5. Bring to a boil for 3 minutes then lower to medium-low heat. Cover and let simmer until flavors have developed, roughly 25-50 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Mix kidney beans and continue to simmer until beans are hot and tender, about 30 minutes more.
  7. Blend sour cream, cilantro, and remaining 1/2 teaspoon cumin in a food processor until smooth. Serve sour cream mixture with chili. Serve amd enjoy!
